1. The Ordinary World

The story begins with the character in their usual routine, unaware of the adventures about to unfold. This is a kind of safe place, or 'comfort zone', which they may or may not want to change. The protagonist's everyday life and routines are presented in a way which endears them to us, and makes us sympathize with their plight. In this stage, we learn important details about our hero, their outlook and personality, backstory, and what they're capable of.
